About me

Jitika
Rajpoot

PhD Student · Chemistry · Purdue University

I'm a PhD student in the Chopra Lab at Purdue University, where I investigate the role of lipid biology in Alzheimer's disease. My work bridges wet-lab biochemistry with computational image analysis — using tools like Imaris and Python to extract meaning from complex microscopy data.

Before Purdue, I completed my BTech in Biotechnology from IIT Guwahati and an MS in Biochemistry & Cell Biology.

What I study

Research Focus

My work sits at the intersection of lipid biochemistry and neurodegeneration, using both experimental and computational approaches to understand Alzheimer's disease.

Lipid Dysregulation in Alzheimer's

Investigating how disruptions in lipid metabolism and membrane composition in the brain contribute to the onset and progression of Alzheimer's disease.

Lipid Biology

Lipid–Protein Interactions

Studying how specific lipids interact with key Alzheimer's proteins — amyloid-β and tau — and how these interactions influence aggregation, toxicity, and disease progression.

Neurodegeneration

Computational Image Analysis

Building Python pipelines interfacing with Imaris to automate segmentation and quantification of fluorescence microscopy images from brain tissue at scale.
